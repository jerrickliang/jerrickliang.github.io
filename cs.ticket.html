Ticket Transaction System

Outline

Over a three-month period, we updated the old campaign website and focused on creating a ticket transaction process to allow users to purchase tickets and fill out ticket information.



Project Background
Annual Conference is the biggest event in Toastmasters (NGO) history, so there will be a lot of members to attend.
In the past, TM members mainly purchased tickets through a contact person, but due to a shortage of workforce for a long time, there were often long queues for ticket purchase, so the organization hopes to solve this problem.
Why did we start?
It would be nice if there was a transaction system that could help the organization deal with the complexity of ticket purchasing and ticket allocation, and at the same time, alleviate the drawbacks of purchasing tickets over the years.
Define Problem & Context
Ticket purchasers are mainly representatives of each division, and therefore have the following characteristics.

How did we do first?

Define our main effort

Since the cash flow system is costly, we decided to focus on developing the whole ticket payment journey.

Competitive analysis

After comparing different third-party payment systems, we decided to use the low-cost Blue New Gold Stream and link the Blue New API to pay for different tickets.



Solution space

1. Ticket purchase

We have designed the ticket selection interface, and added a new trial table, so that users can better understand the total amount.

2.Edit tickets

After completing the ticket purchase, the buyers can freely edit and allocate those purchased tickets.

Challenge
During the development phase, there were two big challenges facing us. - Usability and Schedule planning

So, how did we slove these problem?

1. Usability - Let users know where they are

In order to make the ticketing journey smoother, we designed a dashboard to let users know the status of the ticket purchase and to better understand the surroundings they are staying in.

2. Schedule planning

On the other hand, we still want to keep the product sustainable and on-time, so we adopted Phased development, which allows users to purchase tickets first, while focusing on developing the ticket editing page.

In the end,

We launched on time and received much positive feedback. Besides, over 200 tickets have been sold so far, and the sales number is still rising.

This is not only to accomplish our ultimate wish but also to bring more sales results and exposure to the NGO.

What I learned

1. Communication

During the course of the project, I had constant meetings with my teammates to ensure that our goals were the same.

2. Feasibility design

During the development process, we also encountered schedule control issues, so in my self-reflection, I found that the design side often spent a lot of time designing without taking into account the engineering side and subsequent QA testing, resulting in a lack of development time.

Therefore, in the follow-up design, I would think about the project schedule first and consider the design feasibility to ensure that the project can be launched on time.
